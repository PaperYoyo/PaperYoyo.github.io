Prostitution

 Prostitution has most likely been apart of United State history from the beginning, the trade of sex for something in return. With this it was probably only kept to undergroundish activity out of the very public eye and was something not very glorious.
 Martial law was declared in memphis to help combat the rise of venereal disease during war time by temporarily making prostitution legal and requiring prostitutes undergo health checks and treatments with a fee to obtain a certification to be a prostitute. Starting in 1870 prostitution was seen as a way to decrease crime and increase happiness as well as decrease disease in St. Louis. That year it was made legal with requirements by the state for check and registration of the prostitutes to make it a job for them. Just for years later however it was criminalized again. During this time New York from 1868 to 1877 was going though bill after bill to accomplish this same task with much difficulty. Since these times Prostitution has always mostly been kept in the illegal status with some exceptions here and there such as Nevada in present day.

This painting however not exactly being porn did show and expression of sexuality in the 1840s that was not look upon fondly by the American public. With this social backlash nothing really happened until after the civil war, 1860s, which culture did push for more obscene materials.
 As times continued to change the people changed as well. This can be seen with the content of the entertainment that was being provided at the time. During the 1920s advertisements for things such as TVs, radios, cars, and phones were using sex to pull more customers showing a more luxurious lifestyle. This wasn't just the case for advertisements but for TV and movies as well with movie titles like "A Shocking Night," "Luring Lips," "Red Hot Romance," and "Her Purchase Price."

Much of it was directed towards men in a way that objectified sex and especially women. This was the culmination of what was called the "paperback revolution," which started with pocket sized books being sold in drugstores and newsstands in the late 1930s. Full blown magazines for everyone started coming out such as the Confidential, Keyhole, The African Queen, and most notably Playboy. With this revolution by the mid 1950s it was estimated that this business profits were a couple hundred million dollars a year to one billion dollars a year. And in the mid 1950s the House of Representatives is noted as saying that pornography had "moved from the dark side street into the most respectable neighborhoods and establishments in the community."

Rules on Porn

  Through out the existence of pornography and prostitution there has been those that support it and those that wanted to get rid of it. Groups such as feminists, Women's Christian Temperance Movement, Legion of Decency, National Organization of Decent Literature, and clergy men all at some point, if not their entire existence, fighting to reduce and in some cases rectify, by helping those apart of, pornography and prostitution. Even with the number of groups fighting against it thought out time these activities, mostly pornography, have grown and become more accepted by society. Without a large push, except in select cases, society seemed to keep falling towards pornography and prostitution as a way to let go from the thing around them. This can be seen after every major conflict the US was apart of the rise in these materials and even the legalization of prostitution during these times for soldiers of the military.
  Those that did push for more expression of sexual behavior however did exist and the implication can be made that with the increase of sexual expression the increase of pornography and prostitution would soon follow as an act upon what the public was expressing. One such person would be Margret Sanger with her desires to spread awareness and use of birth control and freedom of women's sexuality. Even though she didn't advocate for more pornography or prostitution the ability to decrease the risk of pregnancy after sex is something that until birth control the prostitution business uses abortions to solve. Another factor would have been Alfred Kinsey and the Kinsey report showing how sexual America's habits were behind closed doors. This exposure to America's sexuality in the shadows prompted more open exploration of sexuality in society. Two groups do come to the for front when talking about socialized sex and that would be the gay and bohemian community. Sexuality with these two communities are intertwined to some of the most core aspects of them and this is expressed on how they present themselves to society. This presentation of intertwined sex into their subcultures also pushed people to be more accepted of a sexualized society.
  Even with social pushes one way or another the government still makes the final decision on what is the law of the land and what is accepted legally in society. When it comes to obscenity laws the early 1900s the basis of all the laws we see today come forth everything else becomes industry regulated or case law from the supreme court. Examples of this would be the 1907 chicago censorship laws for films to regulate what would come to the silver screen then prompting a third party group start rating movies and then the hollywood industry taking over in the mid 1900s. Case laws to be looked at for obscenity would include Queen v Hicklin brought forth an obscenity test that would then be used from the 1930s forward. Eventually this case law lost all power by the Roth decision in mid 1950s when obscene was separated from sexual. This decision neither endorsed or restricted pornography but allowed many more books to come out of hollywood that would be considered as such.
